1. Colligative properties are properties of solution which depend on the number of solute particles in the solution irrespective of their nature.

a) Name the four important colligative properties. (2) [MARCH 2010]

Ans: a) The important colligative properties are Relative lowering of vapour pressure, Elevation of boiling point, Depression of freezing point, Osmotic pressure.

b) What happens to the colligative properties when ethanoic acid is treated with benzene? Give reason. (2) [MARCH 2010]

Ans: b) Ethanoic acid (acetic acid) dimerises in benzene. So the no. of particles decreases and hence the colligative properties decreases.

2. Colligative properties can be used to determine the molar mass of solutes in solutions.

a) What do you mean by colligative properties? (1) [MARCH2011]

Ans: a) These are properties which depend on the number of solute particles and not on their nature.

b) For determining the molecular mass of polymers, osmotic pressure is preferred to other properties. Why? (1) [MARCH2011]

Ans: b) Polymers have poor solubility and so their solution is very dilute. The magnitude of osmotic pressure is large even for very dilute solutions. So osmotic pressure measurement is used for the determination of their molar mass.

c) For intravenous injections only solutions with osmotic pressure equal to that of 0.9% NaCl solution is used. Why? (1) [MARCH2011]

Ans: c) This is because our blood cells are isotonic with 0.9% (mass/volume) NaCl solution. So osmosis does not occur if we place the blood cells in this solution. (If the blood cells are placed in NaCl solution with higher or lower concentrations than 0.9 %, they would shrink or swell).

3. Relative lowering of vapour pressure, elevation of boiling point, depression of freezing point and osmotic pressure are important colligative properties of dilute solutions.

a) Relative lowering of vapour pressure of an aqueous dilute solution of glucose is 0.018. What is the mole fraction of glucose in the solution? (2) [SAY 2011]

Ans: a) Relative lowering of Vapour pressure, \(\frac{P_0 - P_s}{P_0} = x_2\), the mole fraction of the solute.
Here relative lowering of vapour pressure of glucose = 0.018. So its mole fraction = 0.018.

b) An aqueous dilute solution of a non-volatile solute boils at 373.052K. Find the freezing point of the solution. For water \(K_b = 0.52 \, K \, kg \, mol^{-1}\) and \(K_f = 1.86 \, K \, kg \, mol^{-1}\). Normal boiling point of water = 373K and normal freezing point = 273K. (3) [SAY 2011]

Ans: b) Here b.p of solution (\(T_b\)) = 373.052 K and that of water (\(T_b^0\)) = 373 K.
Elevation of b.p, \(\Delta T_b = T_b - T_b^0 = 373.052 - 373 = 0.052 \, K\);
We know that, \(\Delta T_b = K_b \cdot m\)
So, molality, \(m = \frac{\Delta T_b}{K_b} = \frac{0.052}{0.52} = 0.1 \, mol \, kg^{-1}\)
The depression in freezing point, \(\Delta T_f = K_f \cdot m = 1.86 \times 0.1 = 0.186 \, K\)
Also, \(\Delta T_f = T_f^0 - T_f\)
So, freezing point of the solution (\(T_f\)) = \(T_f^0 - \Delta T_f = 273 - 0.186 = 272.814 \, K\)

Ans: i) Raoult’s law
